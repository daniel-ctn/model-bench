# Logo & brand identity — notes

> Status: `current`. The ModelBench "Measured-M" identity: a static mark + a
> subtle animated variant, plus the full favicon / app-icon / OG set. Source of
> truth for the geometry is [`components/brand/geometry.ts`](../../components/brand/geometry.ts);
> all raster assets are generated by
> [`scripts/gen-brand-assets.ts`](../../scripts/gen-brand-assets.ts).

## The chosen direction — "Measured-M"

A geometric **M** (the brand initial) drawn as an instrument:

- the **M letterform** carries the name and stays unmistakable down to 16px;
- it stands on a violet **bench / datum rule** — the ledger every session is
  scored against (the product is a *bench*, and a *journal*);
- a violet **measurement tick** rises from the M's valley to a **confidence
  node** — "a reading that settled to its value", the literal picture of
  ModelBench's thesis: *honest, confidence-aware measurement, not vanity
  metrics*.

So the mark is never a bare letter: it encodes *measure a value above a
baseline, with confidence* — and it pointedly rejects the old logo's stock
speedometer (a generic "instrument" cliché that also collided with the studio's
reference example).

**Why it fits**

- **Domain-true.** Bench + datum + confidence reading = exactly what the app
  does (rank the real value of AI models, with sample-size/Wilson-bounded
  honesty), not a generic analytics squiggle.
- **Brand-like & memorable.** A letterform mark reads instantly as
  "ModelBench" and is the most recoverable shape at favicon scale — a closed,
  high-contrast silhouette where bars/whiskers/squiggles turn to mush.
- **Sibling of the type.** Flat-cut, rationalist proportions echo the app's
  display face (Space Grotesk, the "instrument readout" voice).
- **One signature hue, structural.** Violet (`--primary`) is spent only on
  meaning — the datum and the confidence reading — never as decoration. No
  gradients (house rule); atmosphere on the OG card comes from a hairline grid
  and a single soft glow.

### Alternatives explored

Six directions were generated and scored by a 3-lens adversarial panel
(premium-craft / product-fit / technical-viability). Measured-M won every lens
(87 / 87 / 90):

| Direction | Verdict |
| --- | --- |
| **Measured-M monogram** ✅ | Winner — only one strong on all of premium, fit, favicon and animation; degrades to a recognizable favicon *as itself*. |
| Bench-clamp bracket | Runner-up — strong "test bench / harness" story; brackets+sparkline risk genericness and the inner steps blur at 16px. |
| Calibration bracket (Wilson interval) | Most intellectually ownable, but an error-bar reduces to a smudge below ~22px — fails the favicon gate. |
| Bench + ascending bars | Good leaderboard read, but one nudge from a stock bar-chart/equalizer. |
| Head-to-head verdict peaks | Reads as a chart at a glance; the confidence dot is fragile small. |
| Signal-from-noise peak | Rejected — the banned "up-and-to-the-right" growth squiggle, and proposed an illegal infinite pulse. |

Ideas grafted from the runners-up onto the winner: the **confidence node**
("the reading that landed", from the bracket directions) and the option of a
hairline **interval cap** at large sizes (from the calibration direction —
noted as a future enhancement).

## The animation — "a measurement settling to its value"

A one-shot reveal on mount: the **M draws in**, the **bench/datum rule and
tick trace** via `stroke-dashoffset`, then the **confidence node lands** with a
small ease-out pop. Total ≈1.5s, then still. It is a micro-interaction that
narrates the product metaphor, not a motion graphic.

**Why it's lightweight & safe**

- Paint-only properties (`stroke-dashoffset`, `opacity`, `transform`) — no
  filters, blur, canvas, GIF, Lottie, or JavaScript.
- **No layout shift:** every element occupies its final box from frame 0; only
  paint changes.
- **One-shot, no loop.** The persistent surfaces (sidebar, auth) mount once per
  full page load, so it plays once — not on every client navigation, and never
  ambiently in the navbar.
- **Reduced-motion & robust:** the *base* state is the fully-drawn static mark.
  Motion is added only under `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference)`
  and animates **from** hidden with `fill-mode: both`. So reduced-motion,
  unsupported animations, or an animation that simply never plays all leave the
  mark fully visible — it can never be stranded blank. (Defined in
  [`app/globals.css`](../../app/globals.css) under `.mb-anim`.)

## Recommended usage

**Animated** (`animated` prop / `*-animated.svg`) — high-visibility brand
moments where the reveal is seen and adds value:
- the **sidebar header** logo,
- the **auth** screens (login / signup / reset),
- hero / brand-preview surfaces.

**Static** — everywhere repetition, density, or calm matters:
- favicon, browser tab, app icons, OG image (**never animate these**),
- footers, dense dashboard chrome, repeated instances, mobile.
Use `<LogoMark>` / `<Logo>` **without** `animated`, or the non-animated SVGs.

## Colour & background guidance

- **Signature hue:** violet `--primary` — `#976ef1` (dark) / `#7f4bdc` (light).
  Foreground near-white `#f0f2f6` (dark) / ink `#141720` (light).
- **Tile** (`variant="tile"`): white mark on the violet square — the most
  robust treatment on any tab/background; used for all favicons/app icons.
- **Open** (`variant="open"`): foreground M + violet accents; the accent reads
  from the live `--primary`, so it adapts automatically to dark and light.
- The mark is designed to also work **monochrome** (everything inherits one
  colour) — the hierarchy survives by geometry alone.

## Performance & accessibility notes

- The in-app mark is inline SVG (no extra network request); the reveal is
  GPU-cheap and runs once.
- `<LogoMark>` is `aria-hidden` when decorative and exposes `role="img"` +
  `<title>` when given a `title`.
- Generated PNG/ICO are small (favicon ≈ a few KB; OG ≈ 220 KB).

## Regenerating assets

Edit the geometry once and regenerate every static file:

```bash
# 1. change the mark in components/brand/geometry.ts (and .mb-anim CSS if motion changes)
# 2. regenerate the SVG/PNG/ICO outputs
pnpm tsx scripts/gen-brand-assets.ts
```

Requires the `sharp` + `png-to-ico` devDependencies (already installed). PNG/ICO
outputs are **derived — do not hand-edit them.**
